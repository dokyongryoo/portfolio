Cup of Coffee

Overview

LoFi / NoFi Cinema is a short video work exploring everyday space through the lens of deliberate constraint. Shot entirely on the KODAK Charmera - a keychain-sized digital camera designed as much as an accessory as a tool - the piece uses the camera's built-in black and white filter and its inherent low resolution to strip familiar environments down to texture, light, and shape. The work is aimed at an audience open to lo-fi and experimental video aesthetics, where limitation becomes the medium itself.

Concept and Inspiration

The central impulse was a cultivation of spatial awareness. The University of Chicago John W. Boyer Center in Paris became the subject: its architectural geometry, the rhythm of people getting coffee, the way textures of structural objects usually go unnoticed. The lo-fi quality of the Charmera flattens the images into grainy black and white, removing the distracting clarity of high-definition footage, and forcing attention onto form and composition.

Footage was intentionally limited to the Paris Center and the Maison des étudiants de l'Asie du Sud Est. While broader ambitions to shoot on the streets of Paris were constrained by circumstance, this boundary ultimately tightened the focus of the piece. The video traces the ritual of getting coffee at the Center, using a daily, unremarkable task as an entry point into the space itself: its corners, its light, and the architectural details that usually exist at the edge of awareness.

Process

The work began with open, observational shooting, carrying the Charmera throughout the Paris Center and clipping anything that felt visually alive, particularly the mundane: storage lockers, a couch cushion, the geometry of a stairwell. The editing process in iMovie leaned into layering: clips were stitched together and opacity was adjusted across overlapping segments, creating moments where two spaces briefly coexist before one fades away. This layering technique was the primary formal decision, allowing the video to feel less like documentation and more like a slow accumulation of spatial memory.
