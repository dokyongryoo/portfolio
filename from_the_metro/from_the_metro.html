From the Metro

Overview

From the Metropolitan is an interactive 3D video installation built in New Art City, presenting lo-fi footage of Paris mapped onto a collection of three-dimensional objects. Six video clips are distributed across distinct forms, with texture mapped onto shape, and movement mapped onto structure. Captured on the KODAK Charmera's inherently gritty, low-resolution sensor, the piece invites viewers to reposition themselves within the space for a fragmented, immersive experience of the city.

Viewers are encouraged to move through the New Art City space and situate themselves relative to each object, so that the experience shifts depending on where they stand.

Concept & Inspiration

The inspiration was to explore lo-fi visual texture and sonic texture simultaneously, and to examine what happens when the texture, audio, and form are deliberately misaligned. The Charmera's grainy black and white output, became materialized as its gritty quality was mapped directly onto three-dimensional form, turning texture into structure. The piece asks whether the roughness of a surface - visual or sonic - can carry the same weight as the subject it depicts.

The disconnect between audio and image is intentional. Sound does not illustrate what is shown; instead it creates friction, drawing attention to the gap between what is seen and what is heard, and to the textural qualities of both.

Process & Edited Videos

Clips were shot throughout Paris on the Charmera, edited on iMovie into six separate videos based on broad themes, and mapped onto six distinct objects within the New Art City space:

1. Metro (rectangle - primary object): The largest form houses the other objects within it and plays footage of boarding and exiting the metro. As the structural container of the piece, it sets movement - the act of arriving and departing - as the entry point that underlies everything else.

Video 1: Metro

2. Window (sphere): Mapped onto a secondary sphere within the primary object, this clip collects views from both the metro window and from walking, placing two modes of transit in visual dialogue without resolving which is which.

Video 2: Window

3. Textures (sphere): The final object houses a series of clips focused on gritty surfaces: the couch from the Paris Center, cobblestone paths from the streets of Paris. Different contexts captured by the same camera collapsed into a single, continuous, and grainy texture.

Video 3: Textures

4. Panthéon (cylinder): Footage of the column and pillar architecture alongside the central sphere grounds the piece in one of Paris's most recognizable sites, offering the most reality-anchored moment in the installation. Mapped onto a cylinder, like the Panthéon’s columns at its entrance.

Video 4: Panthéon

5. Tiles (dodecahedron): A wall of tiles seen at a metro station in Paris, reduced to pattern and repetition through the Charmera's low resolution, becomes almost entirely abstract.

Video 5: Tiles

6. Walk (sphere): Walking clips from different locations - down the stairs, on the street - are mapped together, and mismatched audio with movement creates a dissociation between sound and place. The continuous forward motion suggests an unresolvable loop.

Video 6: Walk
